Natural Language Processing Terms

Natural Language Processing with Python is a great entry level book for NLP. I record all basic concepts occurred in this book for reviewing. All codes are written in Python 3. I will keep updating it.

Token

A token is the technical name for a sequence of characters, such as hairy, his or :), that we want to treat as a group. A word token is a particular appearance of a given word in a text. A word type is the unique form of the word as a particular sequence of letters.

It can be used to calculate the lexical richness of a text by below formula. Assume S is the collection of all tokens.

len(set(S))/len(S)

Hapax

Word that occurs only once in a text. Its plural is hapaxes.

Frequency Distribution

A frequency distribution is a collection of items along with their frequency counts (e.g., the words of a text and their frequency of appearance).

Collocation

A collocation is a sequence of words that occur together unusually often. Thus red whine is a collocation, whereas the wine is not.

Bigram

A list of word pairs extracted from a text. Collocations are essentially just bigrams with high frequency.

Word Sense Disambiguation

Working out which sense of a word was intended in a given context.

Pronoun Resolution

Detecting the subjects and objects of verbs from a sentence.

Textual Entailment

A task to judge whether a hypothesis is correct or wrong based on a text automatically.

  • Text: David Golinkin is the editor or author of eighteen books, and over 150 responsa, articles, sermons and books
  • Hypothesis: Golinkin has written eighteen books

Text Corpus or Corpora

A text corpus is a large body of text or linguistic data. Many corpora are designed to contain a careful balance of material in one or more genres.

Average Word Length

Dividing number of character by number of word.

Average Sentence Length

Dividing number of word by number of sentence.

Lexical Diversity Score

It is the number of times each vocabulary item appears in the text on average.

Stylistics

A subject for studying systematic differences between writing genres.

Dispersion Plot

A plot showing location of a word in the text: how many words from the beginning it appears.
